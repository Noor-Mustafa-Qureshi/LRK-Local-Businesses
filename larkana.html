Larkana is a city located in the Sindh province of Pakistan. It is the 15th largest city of Pakistan by population. It is called the city of Holy Alams by Shia Muslims due to the greatest number of Holy Alams as compared to other cities or regions of the world. It is home to the Indus Valley civilization site Mohenjo-daro. The historic Indus River flows in east and south of the city.

The city is located within Larkana District. Formerly known as "Chandka", Larkana is located on the south bank of the Ghar canal, about 40 miles (64 km) south of the town Shikarpur, and 36 miles (58 km) northeast of Mehar. According to the 2017 Census of Pakistan, its population is 490,508. Therefore, it is the fourth most populated city of Sindh province after Karachi, Hyderabad, and Sukkur. Moreover, Larkana city has been placed as fifteenth largest of Pakistan during Census 2017.

A Guide to the Best Places to Visit in Larkana

1- Mohenjo-daro

Mohenjo-daro, one of the most significant archaeological sites of the ancient Indus Valley Civilization, is located just a short drive from Larkana. Explore the well-preserved ruins of this ancient city that dates back over 4,000 years. The Great Bath, the Granary, and the iconic Dancing Girl statue are some of the highlights that provide insight into the advanced civilization that once thrived here.

2- Shahi Bazaar

Immerse yourself in the vibrant local culture by visiting Shahi Bazaar. This bustling market offers a sensory delight with its colorful stalls, aromatic spices, traditional crafts, and authentic local cuisine. Whether you're looking for souvenirs or trying to savor the flavors of Sindh, Shahi Bazaar is a must-visit spot.

3- Chandka Medical College

For history and architecture enthusiasts, a visit to Chandka Medical College is a treat. The majestic building, a blend of British and Mughal architectural styles, is a sight to behold. Take a leisurely stroll through its lush gardens and admire the intricate designs on the walls and ceilings.

4- Garhi Khuda Bakhsh

Pay homage to the Bhutto family legacy by visiting the mausoleum of Zulfikar Ali Bhutto and Benazir Bhutto in Garhi Khuda Bakhsh. The site holds immense political and emotional significance for Pakistan and offers a glimpse into the country's modern history.
